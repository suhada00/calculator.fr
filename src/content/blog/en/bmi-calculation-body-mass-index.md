The **Body Mass Index (BMI)** is an internationally recognized metric used by doctors, nutritionists, and health organizations (like the WHO) to evaluate a person's body mass. It is a quick and simple screening tool to check if an individual is underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

## The BMI Calculation Formula

Calculating your BMI requires only two measurements: your weight in kilograms and your height in meters.

The mathematical formula is:
```
BMI = Weight (kg) / [Height (m) × Height (m)]
```

### Calculation Example:
If you weigh **70 kg** and stand **1.75 m** tall:
* `Height squared = 1.75 × 1.75 = 3.0625`
* `BMI = 70 / 3.0625 = 22.86 kg/m²`

## Interpreting Your BMI Result

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), adults can classify their corpulence using the following ranges:
* **Below 18.5:** Underweight
* **18.5 to 24.9:** Normal (Healthy Weight)
* **25.0 to 29.9:** Overweight
* **30.0 to 34.9:** Moderate Obesity (Class I)
* **35.0 to 39.9:** Severe Obesity (Class II)
* **40.0 and above:** Morbid Obesity (Class III)

## Important Limitations of BMI

While BMI is an excellent benchmark for population studies, it does not directly measure body fat percentage. It cannot differentiate between muscle mass and fat tissue. For instance, bodybuilders or high-performance athletes often score in the "overweight" or "obese" categories because muscle is denser and heavier than fat, despite having low body fat levels.
