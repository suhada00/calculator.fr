Unlike conversions for weight or length, which scale directly from a shared starting point of zero, temperature conversion requires adjusting both the offset (the freezing/zero point) and the increment size of the degrees. The three primary global scales are Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin.

## The Three Temperature Scales

* **Celsius (°C):** The standard metric scale used worldwide. It is calibrated around water: freezing at **0°C** and boiling at **100°C** under standard sea-level atmospheric pressure.
* **Fahrenheit (°F):** The primary scale in the United States. Under Fahrenheit, water freezes at **32°F** and boils at **212°F**.
* **Kelvin (K):** The scientific thermodynamic scale. It starts at absolute zero (**0 K**), which is the theoretical state of zero molecular motion. 0 K is equivalent to -273.15°C.

## Temperature Conversion Formulas

Converting between these scales requires specific algebraic equations:

* **Celsius to Fahrenheit:**
  ```
  Fahrenheit = (Celsius × 9/5) + 32
  ```
* **Fahrenheit to Celsius:**
  ```
  Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) × 5/9
  ```
* **Celsius to Kelvin:**
  ```
  Kelvin = Celsius + 273.15
  ```

### Conversion Example:
To convert a room temperature of **20°C** into Fahrenheit:
`Fahrenheit = (20 × 1.8) + 32 = 36 + 32 = 68°F`
