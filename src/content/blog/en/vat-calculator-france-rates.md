The **Value Added Tax (VAT)**, known as *Taxe sur la Valeur Ajoutée (TVA)* in France, is a consumption tax added to almost all goods and services sold. For consumers, prices are displayed inclusive of tax (*TTC - Toutes Taxes Comprises*). For businesses, prices are managed net of tax (*HT - Hors Taxes*). Knowing how to convert between the two is vital for commerce.

## French VAT Rates for 2026

The VAT percentage depends on the type of product or service sold in France:
* **Standard Rate (20%):** Applies to the majority of goods and services (e.g., consumer goods, electronics, clothing, vehicles, alcohol, and professional services).
* **Intermediate Rate (10%):** Applies to restaurant meals, passenger transport, hotel accommodation, and home renovation work on buildings older than two years.
* **Reduced Rate (5.5%):** Applies to basic foodstuffs, water, books, tickets for cultural events, and energy-saving home renovations.
* **Super-Reduced Rate (2.1%):** Reserved for prescription medications covered by social security and newspapers/press publications.

## How to Calculate VAT

### 1. Converting Net (HT) to Gross (TTC)
To find the final price including tax:
```
Price (TTC) = Price (HT) × (1 + VAT Rate % / 100)
```
*For the standard 20% rate, multiply the HT price by 1.20.*

### 2. Converting Gross (TTC) to Net (HT)
To extract the base price excluding tax (known as reverse VAT calculation):
```
Price (HT) = Price (TTC) / (1 + VAT Rate % / 100)
```
*For the standard 20% rate, divide the TTC price by 1.20.*

### 3. Finding the Total VAT Amount
```
VAT Amount = Price (TTC) - Price (HT)
```
