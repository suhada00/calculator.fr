Splitting a restaurant bill at the end of a group dinner can easily lead to awkward calculations or math headaches. Between figuring out the appropriate tip for the service and dividing the total amount evenly, having a clear method is vital for smooth dining.

## The Mathematics of Tipping

Tipping and splitting are calculated using simple arithmetic steps:

1. **Calculate the Total Tip:**
   ```
   Tip Amount = Base Bill × (Tip Percentage / 100)
   ```
2. **Calculate the Grand Total:**
   ```
   Total Bill = Base Bill + Tip Amount
   ```
3. **Calculate the Split Amount:**
   ```
   Amount Per Person = Total Bill / Number of People
   ```

### Calculation Example:
If your restaurant bill is **$120**, you wish to tip **15%**, and you are splitting the bill among **4 people**:
* `Tip Amount = $120 × 0.15 = $18`
* `Total Bill = $120 + $18 = $138`
* `Amount Per Person = $138 / 4 = $34.50` (which includes a $4.50 tip contribution per person)

## Global Tipping Customs Around the World

Tipping culture changes dramatically depending on where you travel:
* **France & Europe:** In many European countries, a service charge (usually 10% to 15%) is legally included in the menu prices. Leaving an extra cash tip (*pourboire*) of 5% to 10% is polite but entirely optional.
* **United States:** Tipping is functionally mandatory because restaurant staff wages rely heavily on tips. Expect to pay **15% to 20%** of the bill.
* **Japan & South Korea:** Tipping is not part of the culture and is often considered rude or confusing. Good service is simply expected.
