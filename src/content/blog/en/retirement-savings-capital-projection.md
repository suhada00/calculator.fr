Planning for retirement is one of the most critical long-term financial projects an individual can undertake. Due to structural shifts in state pension systems worldwide, building a personal nest egg through individual savings accounts, pension plans, or investment portfolios is essential to maintain your quality of life in your post-work years.

## 1. The Magic of Compound Interest

When saving for retirement, the most crucial factor is not the amount of money you invest, but the **time horizon** you give that money to grow. This is due to **compound interest**, where the returns your investment earns are reinvested to generate their own returns over time.

### Compound Growth Example:
If you save **$200 per month** starting at age 25, investing in a portfolio with a **5% average annual return**:
* By age 65 (after 40 years of continuous saving), your total out-of-pocket contributions will equal **$96,000**.
* Due to compound interest, your final retirement balance will grow to approximately **$305,000**! The investment earnings represent more than double what you saved.

## 2. Adjusting for Inflation

Because retirement saving is a multi-decade project, you must account for **inflation**. Over time, inflation reduces the purchasing power of your money. A balance of $300,000 in 35 years will buy significantly fewer goods than $300,000 would today. Accurate financial tools adjust retirement balances for inflation, expressing your future wealth in "today’s dollars."

## 3. Estimating Your Income Replacement Gap

Your replacement rate is the percentage of your pre-retirement income that you need to maintain your lifestyle. On average, retirees require 70% to 80% of their final salary. Since public pensions rarely cover this entire amount, your personal retirement fund is designed to bridge this income gap.
