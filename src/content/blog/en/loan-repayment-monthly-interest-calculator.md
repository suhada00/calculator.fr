Whether you are looking to purchase a car, remodel your home, or cover unexpected personal expenses, taking out a personal loan requires careful planning. The most important metric to analyze is the **monthly repayment**, which determines how borrowing fits into your monthly household budget.

## The Monthly Repayment Formula

Standard fixed-rate amortizing loans use a financial equation to spread payments evenly across the term. The formula incorporates the principal, the nominal interest rate, and the total number of monthly payments.

The formula is:
```
M = [P × (r / 12)] / [1 - (1 + r / 12)^(-n)]
```
Where:
* **M:** Monthly payment amount.
* **P:** Principal (loan amount).
* **r:** Annual interest rate (in decimal format, e.g., 0.05 for 5%).
* **n:** Total number of monthly payments.

## Understanding Amortization

Each monthly payment is divided into two distinct components:
* **Interest Paid:** The cost charged by the lender, calculated monthly against the remaining principal balance. Early in the loan term, interest represents a larger share of your monthly payment.
* **Principal Repaid:** The portion of the payment that actually reduces your outstanding loan balance. This share grows larger with each passing month.

## Calculating the Total Cost of the Loan

The total cost of borrowing represents all interest payments accumulated across the life of the loan:
```
Total Cost = (Monthly Payment × n) - Principal
```

### Calculation Example:
If you borrow **$15,000** for **36 months** at a **5% interest rate**:
* The calculated monthly payment is **$449.56**.
* The total amount repaid over 3 years is `$449.56 × 36 = $16,184.16`.
* The net cost of the loan (interest) is **$1,184.16**.
