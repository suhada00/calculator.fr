In a globalized world, we frequently encounter different systems of measurement. While the majority of countries rely on the **Metric system**, the United States and a few other regions use the **Imperial system** for everyday measurements of length and distance.

## The Metric System (SI)

The metric system is a base-10 system, which makes scaling between units straightforward through decimal shifts:
* **Millimeter (mm):** `1 mm = 0.1 cm`
* **Centimeter (cm):** `1 cm = 0.01 m`
* **Meter (m):** The base unit of length.
* **Kilometer (km):** `1 km = 1,000 m`

## The Imperial System

The imperial system does not scale by 10s, making manual conversions more complex:
* **Inch (in):** `1 in = 2.54 cm`
* **Foot (ft):** `1 ft = 12 in = 30.48 cm`
* **Yard (yd):** `1 yd = 3 ft = 91.44 cm`
* **Mile (mi):** `1 mi = 1,760 yd = 1.609 km`

## How to Convert Between Systems

Converting between units requires multiplying the source value by a standardized conversion rate.
For example, to convert a person's height of **5 feet 10 inches** (70 inches) into centimeters:
`70 × 2.54 = 177.8 cm`
