Purchasing a home is one of the most significant financial commitments you will make in your lifetime. To make a successful purchase, securing a mortgage is almost always necessary. Before you begin viewing properties, it is essential to determine your maximum borrowing capacity and estimate how interest rates and insurance will shape your monthly budget.

## 1. Components of a Mortgage Payment

A comprehensive mortgage payment typically consists of two main elements:
* **Principal & Interest (P&I):** The baseline cost of repaying the borrowed capital alongside the bank's interest fee, typically amortized over 15, 20, or 25 years.
* **Borrower Insurance:** Mandatory in many European jurisdictions (such as France), insurance covers the debt in the event of death, disability, or loss of employment. It can add significantly to the monthly cost.

## 2. Debt-to-Income (DTI) Limits

Lenders apply strict rules to ensure borrowers do not overextend financially.
* In France, the High Council for Financial Stability (HCSF) mandates that a household's total debt payments (including mortgage, insurance, and existing loans) cannot exceed **35%** of their gross or net taxable income.
* The maximum standard amortization period is capped at **25 years** (which can be extended to 27 years for new constructions or properties requiring major renovations).

## 3. The Power of a Down Payment

A solid down payment (*apport personnel*) is crucial for loan approval. Generally, a minimum of **10%** of the property price is required to cover transaction costs like notary fees and registration taxes. A larger down payment reduces your loan-to-value (LTV) ratio, leading to lower monthly payments and better interest rate offers.
