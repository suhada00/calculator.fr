Clearance sales, seasonal markdowns, and promotional events (like Black Friday) are perfect opportunities to secure bargains. However, stores usually display promotional discounts as percentages (e.g., "30% off" or "save 45%"), leaving it up to you to figure out the actual price at the register.

## The Math of Calculating a Discount

Finding the final price of a discounted item involves three basic steps:

1. **Calculate the Savings Amount:**
   ```
   Savings = Original Price × (Discount Percentage / 100)
   ```
2. **Calculate the Discounted Price:**
   ```
   Discounted Price = Original Price - Savings
   ```
3. **Apply Sales Tax (If Applicable):**
   ```
   Final Price = Discounted Price × (1 + Tax Rate % / 100)
   ```

### Calculation Example:
If you buy a jacket originally priced at **$80** with a **30% discount**:
* `Savings = $80 × (30 / 100) = $24`
* `Discounted Price = $80 - $24 = $56`
You save $24 and pay $56.

## Stacked or Double Discounts

In retail, you might encounter stacked discounts, such as "an extra 20% off already discounted items." 

It is important to remember that **percentage discounts do not add up directly**. A 30% discount followed by a 20% discount is not a 50% discount. The second markdown applies to the already discounted price, not the original price:
* Original Price: $100
* First 30% discount reduces the price to $70
* Second 20% discount applies to $70: `70 × 0.8 = $56` (resulting in a total net discount of 44%).
